The bottom line

In hand today: intake (drafts the booth file from the 3D handoff + flags size disagreements), the uniform template generator, the auto-matched client spec sheet, the AI proofer, and SEE-owned proof sheets with a dated client sign-off + tracking log — proven end-to-end on a real booth (Mama's Creations), our build example.
Only piece left: the live client portal that hosts all of the above so clients self-serve uploads + approvals — the separate funded project below.
Separate funded project, last: the live client portal with lock + add-on payment — it only pays off once the steps above are proven, and needs its own engineering, hosting, and payment setup.

The key insight: uniform templates fix the format; making sure nothing is missed and finishes are right is fixed by standardizing what we capture up front. We're doing both — that's why this works where past attempts stalled.
